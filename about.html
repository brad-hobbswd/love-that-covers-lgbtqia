About the Author

Bradley Hobbs is a writer, teacher, and advocate for healing, grace, and inclusion. With a heart for those who have felt unseen or unloved, his work speaks into places of silence and restores a sense of belonging through truth and compassion.

His journey has been shaped through seasons of silence, deep searching, and personal healing. Through those moments, he has learned the importance of listening for the voice of God and allowing truth to rebuild what life and experience once unsettled.

Brad holds a degree in Information Technology and Business Management and is currently pursuing a master’s in education, specializing in Preschool through 5th Grade. His passion for teaching began through his son, Gabriel, whose life and energy inspired him to step into the classroom and invest in the lives of children with patience, care, and purpose.

His life has included both beautiful and difficult seasons. Through experiences of heartbreak, personal loss, and church hurt, he turned toward God for healing and restoration. Those moments became the foundation for his writing, shaping a message rooted in grace, truth, and renewal.

In addition to Love That Covers, Brad continues to write devotionals and works for readers of all ages, including children. His mission is to help others encounter the love of Jesus, understand their identity, and walk confidently in truth without fear or shame.

Through every page, his desire remains the same: that every reader would know they are fully seen, deeply loved, and never beyond the reach of God’s mercy.

You are seen. You are known. You are loved without condition.
